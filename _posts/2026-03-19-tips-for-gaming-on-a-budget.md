Gaming can get expensive fast — especially when you're into sports titles that drop 
a new edition every year. But you don't need to break the bank to enjoy great games. 
Here's how I keep costs down.

## Wait for the Annual Sale
FC 26, Madden, and NBA 2K all drop significantly in price within a few months of 
release. If you can hold out, you can grab them at half price or less. Patience is 
a skill — both in gaming and in saving money.

## Buy Last Year's Edition
NBA 2K25 and Madden 25 still have active communities and are nearly identical to 
the new versions in many ways. If you're on a budget, last year's game at 80% off 
is often the smarter move.

## Game Pass and PS Plus
Call of Duty is available on Game Pass, which means you can play it without buying 
it outright. If you're not already subscribed to a game service, the value is hard 
to argue with — especially for multiplayer-heavy games like CoD.

## Trade-In or Buy Used
Physical copies of Spider-Man 2, Madden, and NBA 2K can be found used at great prices. 
Trade in games you've finished to offset the cost of new ones.

## Focus on One Game at a Time
Instead of buying everything at launch, pick one game and really dig into it. 
CoD alone can keep you busy for months if you let it. Get your money's worth 
before moving on!

Smart gaming is still great gaming. 🎮
